1. Business Overview

Donda ERP serves diamond manufacturers who purchase rough diamonds from international expos and tenders, import them, and either process them in-house or transfer/sell them to sister companies or third parties.

The Business Cycle

The management team attends diamond expos held in various countries (Belgium, Botswana, South Africa, Canada, etc.). Each expo showcases approximately 600 lots, where each lot contains a single rough diamond or a parcel (group) of rough diamonds identified by a lot/parcel number with its carat weight and price.

The expo runs over multiple weeks. Each day:

  • The team inspects lots and submits bids on hand-picked lots (not all 600)
  • The expo management announces winners
  • Winners pay on the spot
  • Won diamonds are handed to courier/transport companies (e.g., Ferrari Belgium BVBA, Malca-Amit, Brinks) with the winning party's address
Note: The bidding module itself is out of scope for the current phase. We start from the point where a lot is WON and needs to be tracked through transport → purchase → sales.

Post-Winning Flow

Once a lot is won at the expo:

  • Record the win — Lot details, supplier, parcel numbers, carats, price paid, origin
  • Track inbound shipment — Courier, airway bill, route, expected delivery, KPC certificate
  • Create purchase entry — Import invoice with line items, expenses (freight, duty), auto-create Rough Lot Master records
  • Sell or transfer — Create sales invoice to sister company (inter-company) or third party, with outbound shipment tracking

2. Key Stakeholders & Entities

Expo / Tender Supplier

Example: Bonas Couzyn (Antwerp) NV

Sells rough diamonds through expos/tenders. Issues supplier invoices with parcel details, mining origin, and KPC documentation.

Buying Company

Example: Rainbow Gems (HK) Ltd

The primary buyer. Receives import shipments, creates purchase entries, holds rough diamond inventory.

Sister Company

Example: Donda Exports

Same owner, different country. Receives inter-company transfers with sales invoices. May also sell to third parties.

Transport / Courier

Example: Ferrari Belgium BVBA

Handles secure diamond logistics. Issues House Airway Bills, manages sealed parcels with KPC compliance.

Mining Companies

Examples: Mountain Province, Petra Diamonds, KAO/Storm Mountain

Original source of rough diamonds. Their mine and country determine the origin tracking and KPC requirements.

Third-Party Buyers

External companies purchasing rough or polished diamonds. Standard sales invoices apply.

Key Business Rules

  • A single shipment can contain parcels from multiple supplier invoices and multiple mining origins
  • Each parcel becomes a Rough Lot in inventory with a system-generated lot number
  • Expenses (freight, import duty, other) are allocated proportionally to rough lots based on value
  • Inventory valuation = Purchase price + Allocated expenses
  • Each shipment must have a Kimberley Process Certificate (KPC) for compliance
  • Payment to supplier is typically within 5 working days from invoice date
  • Currency is primarily USD but the system must support multiple currencies with exchange rates
  • Sales can be to sister companies (inter-company transfer) or third parties (regular sale)

Auto Accounting

When a purchase invoice is confirmed, the system auto-generates accounting entries:
  • Debit: Rough Diamond Purchase Account (goods value)
  • Debit: Related Expense Accounts (freight, duty, etc.)
  • Credit: Supplier / Accounts Payable
Inventory is added to Rough Stock at Purchase Value + Allocated Expenses.
